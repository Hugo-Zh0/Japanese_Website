# 🇯🇵 日本語 (Japanese) Dictionary & Spelling Box Generator

Welcome to the Japanese Spelling Box Generator! This is a fast, 100% free, and completely easy to use tool designed to help Japanese language learners and teachers create perfectly formatted writing practice worksheets.

Whether you are practicing Hiragana, Katakana, or Kanji, this tool uses smart algorithms to calculate and generate character boxes you need and formats them onto a print-ready A4 page! With the added bonus of your own dictionary, that you can share or use.

## Why I Created This
Learning Japanese requires a lot of writing practice. While premade worksheets are great, they don't help when you want to study a specific list of vocabulary (like words for an upcoming test). Making custom writing boxes manually in Word or Excel is incredibly tedious and formatting them to fit a page is frustrating. 

I built this website to solve that problem. You simply build your own personal dictionary, click the words you want to study, and the website instantly generates a smart, auto-filling worksheet that you can print right from your browser.

## Key Features
* **Personal Dictionary:** Add and manage your own custom vocabulary list.
* **Smart Auto-Fill:** The generator finds and randomizes your words to ensure no space is wasted on the A4 page.
* **Quick Add Word Bank:** Build worksheets in seconds by simply clicking words from your dictionary.
* **100% Private & Offline:** Your words are saved locally in your browser. No accounts, no logins, and no data sent to servers.
* **Import / Export:** Easily backup your dictionary or share it with students/classmates using CSV or JSON files.


## How to Use the Website

### Step 1: Build Your Dictionary
1. Go to the **Dictionary** tab.
2. Type in the **English** meaning, and the Japanese reading (**Hiragana, Katakana, and/or Kanji**). 
   * *Tip: If a word has multiple readings, separate them with a slash (e.g., `先生 / 教師`).*
3. Click **Add Word**. The word will appear in your scrollable list below.

    **note:** You can also download the template and prefill the words to be imported back onto the website, where you can also update and export as an JSON file that can be shared.

### Step 2: Build Your Worksheet
1. Switch to the **Generator** tab.
2. Use the **Quick Add Word Bank** on the right. Search for the words you want to practice and click them. They will automatically be added to your worksheet list and checked off.
3. Choose your **Mode** (Do you want to practice writing Hiragana? Kanji? Both?).
4. Choose how many times you want each word to **Repeat**.

### Step 3: Generate & Print
1. Click the red **Generate** button to preview your worksheet at the bottom of the screen.
2. Check the **Auto-fill page** toggle if you want the app to automatically repeat your words to fill all the empty space on the paper.
3. Click the dark **Print** button!


## Troubleshooting & FAQ

**"I refreshed the page and my words are still there. Where is this saving?"**
Your dictionary is saved in your browser's "Local Storage." It will stay there until you explicitly clear your browser's history/site data.

**"How do I use this on another computer?"**
Because it saves to your browser, it won't automatically sync to your phone or another PC. To move your words, go to the **Dictionary tab**, click **Export**, and email or message that downloaded file to yourself. On your new computer, click **Import** and upload that file.

**"The printed page looks blank or the boxes are missing!"**
When the print dialog opens, look for your printer settings (often under "More Settings") and make sure:
1. Paper size is set to **A4**.
2. Scale is set to **Default** or **100%**.
3. Check the box for **"Print Background Graphics"** (this ensures the thick borders of the boxes print correctly).

**"I'm getting a yellow 'missing word' alert."**
If you manually type a word into the Generator text area that hasn't been added to your Dictionary tab first, the app won't know how many boxes to draw! Click the "Add them now" button inside the yellow alert to quickly add the missing Japanese translations.

**"The generator drew 4 boxes, but I only wanted 3."**
Sometimes Japanese punctuation or split readings can confuse the box calculator. You can manually delete or add a box to any word on the generated worksheet by hovering over the word. Click the **Red X** to remove a box, or the **Green +** to add one. The app will remember your fix for next time!
